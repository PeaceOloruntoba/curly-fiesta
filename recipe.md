# Complete Nigerian Meals Guide with Recipes

## Breakfast Meals

### 1. Akara (Bean Cakes)

*Description:* Akara are delicious deep-fried bean cakes made from peeled black-eyed peas blended with onions and peppers. These golden-brown fritters have a crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior, commonly enjoyed as a breakfast staple across West Africa.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 2 cups black-eyed peas (peeled)
- 1 medium onion
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Vegetable oil for deep frying
- Water as needed

*Instructions:*
1. Soak the black-eyed peas in water for 30 minutes to soften the skin
2. Rub the beans between your palms to remove the skins, then wash thoroughly
3. Blend the peeled beans with onions, peppers, and minimal water until smooth and fluffy
4. Add salt and whisk the batter vigorously for 3-5 minutes to incorporate air
5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (180°C)
6. Using a spoon, scoop portions of batter and gently drop into hot oil
7. Fry for 3-4 minutes until golden brown, turning occasionally
8. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels
9. Serve hot with pap, custard, or bread

### 2. Moi Moi

*Description:* Moi Moi is a steamed bean pudding made from blended black-eyed peas, creating a protein-rich, savory cake with a smooth texture. It's often enriched with boiled eggs, fish, or corned beef and wrapped in leaves or foil before steaming.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 3 cups black-eyed peas (peeled)
- 2 medium onions
- 3 red bell peppers
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 3 stock cubes
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 cups warm water
- Optional: boiled eggs, smoked fish, corned beef

*Instructions:*
1. Peel the black-eyed peas using the same method as Akara
2. Blend beans with onions, bell peppers, and scotch bonnet until very smooth
3. Pour mixture into a bowl and add vegetable oil, stock cubes, and salt
4. Gradually add warm water, stirring until you achieve a thick, pourable consistency
5. Prepare your containers: leaves, foil pockets, or ramekins greased with oil
6. Pour batter into containers, filling 3/4 full
7. Add optional ingredients like egg slices or fish pieces
8. Steam in a large pot with 2 inches of water for 45-60 minutes
9. Check doneness by inserting a skewer; it should come out clean
10. Allow to cool slightly before unwrapping and serving

### 3. Yam & Egg Sauce

*Description:* A hearty breakfast combination featuring boiled or fried yam served with a rich, spicy egg sauce made from scrambled eggs cooked with tomatoes, peppers, and onions. This satisfying meal provides both carbohydrates and protein.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 1 medium yam tuber (peeled and cut into chunks)
- 6 eggs
- 4 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (chopped)
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 stock cubes
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*
1. Peel yam, cut into 2-inch chunks, and wash thoroughly
2. Boil yam in salted water for 20-25 minutes until tender (or fry if preferred)
3. For the sauce: heat oil in a pan over medium heat
4. Sauté chopped onions until translucent
5. Add blended tomatoes and peppers, cook for 10 minutes until oil rises
6. Season with stock cubes and salt
7. Beat eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt
8. Pour eggs into the tomato sauce and scramble gently
9. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally
10. Serve hot sauce over boiled or fried yam

### 4. Pap & Akara

*Description:* A classic Nigerian breakfast pairing of smooth, warm pap (ogi/akamu) - a fermented corn pudding - served alongside crispy akara. The creamy, slightly sour pap perfectly complements the savory, crunchy bean cakes.

*Recipe Details:*

*Pap Ingredients:*
- 2 cups fermented corn flour (ogi powder)
- 6 cups water (divided)
- Sugar to taste
- Milk (optional)

*Akara Ingredients:* (See Akara recipe above)

*Instructions for Pap:*
1. Mix ogi powder with 2 cups cold water to form a smooth paste
2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot
3. Gradually pour the ogi paste into boiling water, stirring continuously
4. Reduce heat to low and continue stirring for 5-7 minutes until thickened
5. Add sugar and milk if desired
6. Serve hot in bowls alongside freshly fried akara

*Instructions for Akara:* Follow recipe #1 above

### 5. Plantain Porridge

*Description:* A delicious one-pot meal made with ripe or unripe plantains cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce with vegetables, fish, or meat. This comfort food is both filling and nutritious, offering a perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 4 ripe plantains (peeled and cubed)
- 3 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers
- 1/2 cup palm oil or vegetable oil
- 1 cup smoked fish or beef
- 2 stock cubes
- 1 teaspoon crayfish (ground)
- 1 cup spinach or scent leaves (chopped)
- 3 cups water
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*
1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat
2. Sauté onions until soft, then add blended tomatoes and peppers
3. Cook tomato mixture for 10 minutes until oil separates
4. Add water, stock cubes, crayfish, and bring to a boil
5. Add cubed plantains and smoked fish/meat
6. Cook covered for 15-20 minutes until plantains are tender
7. Add chopped vegetables and stir gently
8. Cook for another 5 minutes until vegetables wilt
9. Adjust seasoning and serve hot

### 6. Bread & Scrambled Eggs

*Description:* A quick and easy breakfast featuring soft bread slices paired with fluffy scrambled eggs cooked with tomatoes, onions, and peppers. This Western-style breakfast has been fully embraced in Nigerian homes.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 6 eggs
- 4 slices of bread (white or wheat)
- 2 medium tomatoes (diced)
- 1 small onion (diced)
- 1 green bell pepper (diced)
- 2 tablespoons butter or vegetable oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Optional: milk (2 tablespoons)

*Instructions:*
1. Beat eggs in a bowl with milk (if using), salt, and black pepper
2. Heat butter or oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat
3. Sauté onions until soft and translucent
4. Add diced tomatoes and green pepper, cook for 3 minutes
5. Pour in beaten eggs and let sit for 30 seconds
6. Using a spatula, gently fold eggs from the edges to the center
7. Continue folding until eggs are just set but still creamy (2-3 minutes)
8. Remove from heat immediately to prevent overcooking
9. Toast bread if desired and serve eggs on the side or on top

### 7. Yam Porridge (Asaro)

*Description:* Asaro is a pottage dish made from yam cooked until it breaks down into a thick, hearty porridge enriched with palm oil, peppers, and often smoked fish. The yam becomes soft and absorbs all the flavorful seasonings.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 1 medium yam tuber (peeled and cubed)
- 1/2 cup palm oil
- 3 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 cup smoked fish or dried fish
- 2 stock cubes
- 1 tablespoon ground crayfish
- 1 cup spinach (optional)
- 4 cups water
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*
1. Place cubed yam in a pot with 4 cups water and salt
2. Boil for 10 minutes until slightly tender
3. Add palm oil, blended tomatoes, peppers, and onions
4. Add stock cubes, crayfish, and smoked fish
5. Cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to break down yam
6. Use a wooden spoon to mash some yam pieces against the pot side
7. Continue cooking until you achieve desired consistency (thick porridge)
8. Add spinach if using and cook for 3 more minutes
9. Adjust seasoning and serve hot

### 8. Pancakes (Nigerian Style)

*Description:* Nigerian pancakes are thicker and less sweet than American pancakes, often made with less sugar and sometimes incorporating spices like nutmeg. They can be enjoyed plain or with eggs and sausages.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 3 eggs
- 1.5 cups milk
- 3 tablespoons sugar
- 1/4 cup melted butter or vegetable oil
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
- Pinch of salt
- Butter for frying

*Instructions:*
1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, nutmeg, and salt
2. In another bowl, beat eggs then add milk and melted butter
3. Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and mix until just combined
4. Let batter rest for 10 minutes (it should be thick but pourable)
5. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and brush with butter
6. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake
7. Cook for 2-3 minutes until bubbles form on surface
8. Flip and cook other side for 2 minutes until golden
9. Serve warm with honey, jam, or savory accompaniments

### 9. Fried Plantain & Beans

*Description:* A beloved combination of sweet fried plantain and savory beans cooked in a rich tomato sauce. The sweetness of the plantain perfectly balances the hearty, protein-rich beans.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*

For Beans:
- 2 cups brown beans or black-eyed peas
- 1/2 cup palm oil
- 3 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers
- 2 stock cubes
- Salt to taste

For Plantain:
- 3 ripe plantains (peeled and sliced)
- Vegetable oil for frying
- Pinch of salt

*Instructions:*

For Beans:
1. Wash beans and cook with enough water until tender (45-60 minutes)
2. In a separate pot, heat palm oil and sauté onions
3. Add blended tomatoes and peppers, cook for 10 minutes
4. Add cooked beans with some cooking liquid
5. Season with stock cubes and salt
6. Simmer for 15 minutes until sauce thickens

For Plantain:
1. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat
2. Slice plantains diagonally about 1/2 inch thick
3. Fry plantain slices for 2-3 minutes per side until golden
4. Remove and drain on paper towels
5. Sprinkle with salt if desired

Serve beans and fried plantain together on a plate.

### 10. Custard & Bread

*Description:* A simple, comforting breakfast of smooth, warm custard served with bread. The custard is made from custard powder mixed with milk and sugar, creating a creamy drink or pudding consistency.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 4 tablespoons custard powder
- 3 cups milk (or water)
- 3 tablespoons sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
- 4 slices of bread
- Butter (optional)

*Instructions:*
1. Mix custard powder with 1/2 cup cold milk to form a smooth paste
2. Heat remaining 2.5 cups milk in a pot until warm (not boiling)
3. Add sugar to the warm milk and stir until dissolved
4. Gradually pour custard paste into warm milk, stirring constantly
5. Continue stirring over medium heat for 3-5 minutes until thickened
6. Add vanilla extract if using
7. Remove from heat and let cool slightly
8. Pour into cups and serve warm with bread
9. Butter bread if desired

### 12. Oat Meal with Fruit

*Description:* A healthy breakfast option featuring creamy oatmeal cooked with milk and topped with fresh tropical fruits like banana, pawpaw (papaya), and pineapple. This meal provides fiber, vitamins, and sustained energy.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 1 cup rolled oats
- 2 cups milk (or water, or combination)
- 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
- Pinch of salt
- 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)
- Fresh fruits: banana slices, pawpaw cubes, pineapple chunks
- Optional toppings: nuts, raisins, coconut flakes

*Instructions:*
1. Bring milk (or water) to a gentle boil in a pot
2. Add oats, salt, and cinnamon, stirring to combine
3. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally
4. Oats should be soft and creamy; add more liquid if needed
5. Remove from heat and stir in honey or sugar
6. Pour into bowls and let cool for 2 minutes
7. Top with assorted fresh fruit slices
8. Add optional toppings as desired
9. Serve warm

### 13. Fried Yam & Pepper Sauce

*Description:* Crispy fried yam chunks served with a spicy, tangy pepper sauce made from fresh tomatoes and scotch bonnet peppers. This popular street food and breakfast option is both satisfying and flavorful.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*

For Yam:
- 1 medium yam tuber (peeled and cut)
- Vegetable oil for deep frying
- Salt to taste

For Pepper Sauce:
- 4 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 3 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 large onion (diced)
- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 stock cubes
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*

For Yam:
1. Peel yam and cut into 1/2-inch thick slices or chunks
2. Soak in salted water for 5 minutes
3. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (180°C)
4. Drain yam and pat dry with paper towels
5. Fry yam in batches for 7-10 minutes until golden and crispy
6. Drain on paper towels and sprinkle with salt

For Pepper Sauce:
1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat
2. Sauté diced onions until soft
3. Add blended tomatoes and peppers
4. Cook for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally
5. Season with stock cubes and salt
6. Simmer until sauce thickens slightly
7. Serve hot alongside fried yam

### 15. Masa (Rice Cakes)

*Description:* Masa are fluffy, spongy rice cakes popular in Northern Nigeria, made from fermented rice batter and cooked in special indented pans. They have a slightly sour taste and soft texture, often enjoyed with sugar or pepper sauce.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 3 cups rice (preferably short-grain)
- 1/2 cup cooked rice
- 1 tablespoon yeast
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon potash (kanwa) dissolved in water
- Warm water as needed
- Vegetable oil for greasing

*Instructions:*
1. Soak rice overnight, then drain
2. Blend soaked rice with cooked rice and enough water to make a smooth batter
3. Pour into a bowl and add yeast and 1 tablespoon sugar
4. Cover and let ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight
5. Add dissolved potash and mix well (batter should be thick but pourable)
6. Heat masa pan or small non-stick pan with indentations
7. Grease with oil
8. Pour batter into indentations, filling halfway
9. Cover and cook for 3-4 minutes until set and golden underneath
10. Flip and cook other side briefly
11. Serve with sugar, honey, or pepper sauce

## Lunch & Dinner Meals

### 16. Jollof Rice

*Description:* Nigeria's most famous dish, Jollof rice is a vibrant one-pot rice meal cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce with aromatic spices. The rice achieves a beautiful orange-red color and slightly smoky flavor, often served at parties and celebrations.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
- 5 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 2 red bell peppers (blended)
- 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 large onion (sliced)
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- 3 stock cubes
- 2 teaspoons curry powder
- 1 teaspoon thyme
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon ginger powder
- 3-4 cups chicken or beef stock
- Salt to taste
- Bay leaves (optional)

*Instructions:*
1. Wash rice thoroughly and set aside to drain
2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat
3. Fry sliced onions until soft, then remove half for later
4. Add tomato paste and fry for 2 minutes
5. Add blended tomatoes and peppers, cook for 15-20 minutes until oil rises
6. Add curry powder, thyme, garlic, ginger, stock cubes, and bay leaves
7. Pour in stock and bring to a boil
8. Add rice, stir well, and reduce heat to low
9. Cover tightly with foil then lid to trap steam
10. Cook for 25-30 minutes without stirring
11. Check rice; if needed, add more stock and cook until tender
12. Add reserved fried onions on top
13. Let steam for 5 minutes, then fluff and serve

### 17. Efo Riro (Vegetable Soup)

*Description:* A rich, flavorful Yoruba spinach soup loaded with assorted meats, fish, and traditional spices. Efo Riro combines leafy greens with a thick, peppery sauce enriched with palm oil and locust beans.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 4 cups spinach or green amaranth (chopped)
- 1/2 cup palm oil
- 1 lb assorted meat (beef, tripe, cow skin)
- 1/2 lb smoked fish
- 3 medium tomatoes (blended)
- 2 red bell peppers (blended)
- 3 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
- 1 tablespoon locust beans (iru)
- 3 stock cubes
- 2 cups stock from meat
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*
1. Cook assorted meats with stock cubes and onions until tender
2. Heat palm oil in a large pot over medium heat
3. Add locust beans and fry for 2 minutes
4. Add chopped onions and sauté until soft
5. Pour in blended tomatoes and peppers
6. Cook for 15 minutes until oil floats on top
7. Add cooked meat, smoked fish, and crayfish
8. Pour in meat stock and simmer for 10 minutes
9. Add chopped vegetables and stir gently
10. Cook for 5-7 minutes until vegetables wilt
11. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with fufu, eba, or pounded yam

### 18. Egusi Soup

*Description:* A popular Nigerian soup made from ground melon seeds (egusi) that creates a thick, nutty-flavored soup packed with meat, fish, and leafy vegetables. The egusi adds a rich, unique texture and protein content.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
- 1 lb assorted meat (beef, goat, chicken)
- 1/2 lb dried fish and stockfish (soaked)
- 1/2 cup palm oil
- 4 cups bitter leaf or pumpkin leaves (washed and chopped)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 3 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
- 1 tablespoon locust beans (optional)
- 3 stock cubes
- 4 cups meat stock
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*
1. Cook assorted meats with stock cubes until tender, reserve stock
2. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat
3. Add chopped onions and locust beans, fry for 2 minutes
4. Add blended peppers and cook for 5 minutes
5. Mix ground egusi with 1 cup water to form a paste
6. Add egusi paste to pot, stirring constantly for 5 minutes
7. Gradually add meat stock, stirring to prevent lumps
8. Add cooked meat, fish, stockfish, and crayfish
9. Simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally
10. Add washed vegetables and cook for 5 minutes
11. Adjust seasoning and serve with eba, fufu, or pounded yam

### 19. Ofada Rice & Ayamase Sauce

*Description:* Ofada rice is an unpolished, locally-grown Nigerian rice with a unique aroma, served with Ayamase - a spicy green pepper sauce made with assorted meats and locust beans. This combination is a specialty delicacy with bold flavors.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*

For Ofada Rice:
- 3 cups ofada rice
- Water for boiling
- Salt to taste

For Ayamase Sauce:
- 10-12 green bell peppers (blended)
- 4 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 lb assorted meat (beef, tripe, cow skin, liver)
- 1/2 cup palm oil
- 1 large onion (sliced)
- 3 tablespoons locust beans (iru)
- 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
- 3 stock cubes
- Salt to taste

*Instructions:*

For Rice:
1. Wash ofada rice thoroughly several times to remove dirt
2. Boil with excess water and salt for 25-30 minutes until tender
3. Drain in a sieve and rinse with cold water
4. Steam for 5 minutes to separate grains

For Ayamase:
1. Season and cook assorted meats until tender
2. Heat palm oil until it bleaches slightly (turns clear)
3. Add locust beans and fry for 2 minutes
4. Add sliced onions and fry until brown
5. Pour in blended green peppers and scotch bonnet
6. Cook on high heat for 10 minutes (sauce should be watery)
7. Add cooked meat, crayfish, stock cubes, and salt
8. Simmer for 10-15 minutes
9. Serve sauce over ofada rice wrapped in leaves

### 20. Banga Soup

*Description:* A delicious Niger Delta soup made from palm nut extract, creating a rich, oily broth flavored with banga spices. This soup is thick, aromatic, and traditionally served with starch or eba.

*Recipe Details:*

*Ingredients:*
- 2 cups palm nut extract (or 1 can banga soup base)
- 1 lb catfish or beef
- 1/2 lb dried fish
- 2 tablespoons banga spices (or blend: ehuru, uyayak, oburunbebe stick)
- 3 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
- 1 large onion (chopped)
- 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
- 2 stock cubes
- 4 cups water
- Salt to taste
- Scent leaves (optional)

*Instructions:*
1. If using fresh palm nuts: boil until soft, pound and extract juice
2. Season and cook meat/fish until tender
3. In a pot, add palm nut extract and water, bring to a boil
4. Add banga spices tied in a cheesecloth or loose
5. Add blended peppers, onions, and crayfish
6. Simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally
7. Add cooked meat/fish and stock cubes
8. Cook for another 15 minutes until soup thickens
9. Add chopped scent leaves if using
10. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with starch or eba

Name: Edikang Ikong
Description:
A nutritious and flavorful vegetable soup from the Efik and Ibibio people of southeastern Nigeria. This rich soup combines two types of leafy greens (ugu and waterleaf) with assorted meats, fish, and periwinkles in a palm oil-based broth.
Details (Recipe):
Ingredients:

2 cups waterleaf (washed and sliced)
2 cups ugu/pumpkin leaves (washed and sliced)
500g assorted meat (beef, goat meat)
200g stockfish (soaked and shredded)
200g dried fish (deboned)
1 cup periwinkles (washed)
1 cup crayfish (ground)
1 cup palm oil
2 medium onions (diced)
3 Scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
2 seasoning cubes
Salt to taste

Preparation Steps:

Season and cook assorted meat with onions, seasoning cubes, and salt until tender. Reserve the stock.
Add stockfish and dried fish to the meat stock and simmer for 10 minutes.
Heat palm oil in a pot, add diced onions and blended peppers, fry for 3 minutes.
Add the meat, fish, and stock to the palm oil mixture.
Stir in ground crayfish and periwinkles, cook for 5 minutes.
Add waterleaf first, stir and cook for 3 minutes.
Add ugu leaves, stir gently and cook for 2 minutes.
Adjust seasoning to taste and remove from heat.
Serve hot with your choice of swallow (eba, fufu, or pounded yam).

Name: Beans & Plantain (Ewa Riro)
Description:
A popular Nigerian dish combining protein-rich beans with sweet plantains. Ewa Riro is a flavorful bean porridge cooked with peppers, onions, and palm oil, often enjoyed with fried ripe plantains on the side. It's a satisfying comfort food common among the Yoruba people.
Details (Recipe):
Ingredients:

3 cups brown beans (washed)
3 ripe plantains (sliced)
1/2 cup palm oil
1 large onion (chopped)
3-4 fresh tomatoes (blended)
2 red bell peppers (blended)
3 Scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
1/2 cup crayfish (ground)
2 seasoning cubes
Salt to taste
Vegetable oil for frying plantains

Preparation Steps:

Cook beans in a pressure cooker or regular pot with water until soft (about 45 minutes-1 hour).
Heat palm oil in a separate pot, add chopped onions and fry until translucent.
Add blended peppers and tomatoes, fry for 10-15 minutes until oil rises to the top.
Add ground crayfish, seasoning cubes, and salt.
Pour in the cooked beans with some of its water to achieve desired consistency.
Stir well and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing flavors to combine.
Meanwhile, heat vegetable oil in a frying pan and fry plantain slices until golden brown.
Serve the bean porridge hot with fried plantains on the side.
